How ECCD Scoring Works

The Early Childhood Care and Development (ECCD) Checklist measures developmental milestones across 7 domains to evaluate a child's overall developmental progress.

📅 Computed Age Chronological age calculated on the day of the assessment (Assessment Date minus Date of Birth). Since children develop rapidly, all milestones are assessed relative to this precise age group.
📈 Sum of Scaled Scores The sum of scores obtained across all 7 developmental domains (Gross Motor, Fine Motor, Self-Help, Receptive, Expressive, Cognitive, and Social-Emotional). Each domain's score matches the number of milestones successfully demonstrated.
🎯 Standard Score The normalized overall development index scaled based on national childhood development norms.
  • 80 - 119: Average Development
  • 70 - 79: Suggests Slight Delay
  • Below 70: Suggests Significant Delay
  • 120 - 129: Slightly Advanced
  • 130 & above: Highly Advanced

*Note: Chronological age is calculated by subtracting the child's date of birth from the assessment date. Each month is composed of 30 days. No rounding off of months or years.

How to Interpret this Table:
  • Date Tested: The exact date the teacher administered the checklist to the child.
  • Date of Birth: The child's official birth date used as the baseline for all calculations.
  • Computed Chronological Age: Expressed precisely in Years, Months, and Days. Because rapid development occurs in early childhood, this exact age is used to determine which developmental milestone norms (Ages 3.1-4.0, 4.1-5.0, or 5.1-5.11 years) are applicable for scoring.

How to Interpret this Table:
  • Raw Score: Represented inside the cells (e.g. 5-7 or 10). This corresponds to the number of specific milestone indicators marked "Present" in a domain.
  • Scaled Score: The numbers from 1 to 19 in the leftmost column. A scaled score of 10 represents the national norm/average for a child of this exact age group.
  • Highlighting: The green cells highlight your child's actual raw score in each domain and immediately display their corresponding scaled score on the far left.

How to Interpret this Table:

  • Standard Score: The normalized value mapped from the sum of scaled scores. This value indicates the child's overall developmental percentile.
  • Highlighting: The green cell indicates your child's specific sum of scaled scores and maps it directly to their final Standard Score.
